# barents-sea-analysis
Time series analysis of Barents Sea climate variables (T2m, SST, SIC) using EOF/PCA, FFT, and deep learning - YSB575E Course Project, ITU
Conducted as a course project for Data Analysis for Climate Science at Istanbul Technical University, this study presents a data-driven climate analysis of the Barents Sea (2000–2015) investigating the relationships between 2-meter air temperature (T2m), sea surface temperature (SST), and sea ice concentration (SIC). The course focuses on quantitative methods for analyzing climate datasets, and this project applies those methods to examine long-term warming trends, seasonal climatology, anomaly patterns, and atmosphere-ocean coupling in one of the most rapidly changing regions in the Arctic.
Analyzed three key climate variables: T2m, SST, and SIC, over a 15-year period using time series methods. Identified a statistically significant warming trend of +0.12°C/year in air temperature (+1.2°C/decade) and detected a structural break in sea ice concentration after 2005, after which the Barents Sea no longer exhibits a consistent seasonal freeze-up cycle. Performed correlation and lag-correlation analysis revealing strong atmosphere-ocean coupling (R=0.77 for T2m–SST) and direct atmospheric forcing on ice (R=−0.44). Applied FFT power spectrum analysis and signal decomposition using band-pass filtering to confirm the breakdown of the 12-month seasonal ice rhythm, a critical indicator of Arctic climate disruption.
